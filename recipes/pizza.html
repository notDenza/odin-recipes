Classic Pizza Napoletana


Prep30 min
Rise8 hr
Cook10 min
Total~9 hr
Makes4 pizzas

Description :

This Neapolitan pizza is simple, soft, and full of flavor, with a thin charred crust and just a few fresh toppings that let the dough shine.

It comes straight from Naples, and these are the ingredients you will need:

Ingredients :

  • 500 g (4 cups) "00" flour
  • 325 ml (1 ⅓ cups) water, room temperature
  • 3 g (1 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 10 g (2 tsp) salt
  • 1 can (400 g) San Marzano tomatoes
  • 250 g fresh mozzarella, torn
  • 1 handful fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil

Steps :

  1. Dissolve the yeast in the water. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt, then pour in the water and mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough on a clean surface for 10–15 minutes, until smooth and elastic.
  3. Cover the bowl and let the dough rise at room temperature for 8 hours (or overnight in the fridge), until doubled in size.
  4. Divide the dough into 4 equal balls (about 200 g each), cover, and rest for 30 minutes.
  5. Gently stretch each ball by hand into a 10-inch round, keeping a thicker rim around the edge. Avoid using a rolling pin.
  6. Crush the tomatoes with a pinch of salt and spread a thin layer over the dough, leaving the rim bare.
  7. Scatter the torn mozzarella and a few basil leaves on top.
  8. Drizzle lightly with olive oil.
  9. Bake in the hottest possible oven, 500°F (260°C) or higher, for 8–10 minutes, until the crust is puffed and charred in spots.
  10. Serve hot, with a few extra fresh basil leaves on top.
